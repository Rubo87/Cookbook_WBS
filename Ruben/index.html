  1. Soak the beans in cold water the night before, at least 12 hours. Do the same with the pork shoulder and bacon. This helps to soften their flavour and remove any impurities.
  2. The next day, place the drained fabes in a pot with the Asturian compango (the pork shoulder, bacon, chorizo and black pudding) along with an onion, a spoonful of paprika and the saffron threads (add these when we have defoamed the beans) and cover with cold water. These last three ingredients are optional, as I follow the recipe of an Asturian bar that the owner told me she used them and it was great. The traditional recipe does not use them.
  1. The secret for them to be tender is to "scare the beans" 3 times. When it comes to the boil, stop the cooking with a glass of cold water, and repeat this 3 times.
  2. Leave to cook for 2 and a half to 3 hours. Cook over a low heat with the lid on and never use a ladle to stir as this could break the beans.
  3. Once cooked, adjust the salt and remove the compango. We can break it up and add it or serve it separately. I recommend cooking from one day to the next as they have a better flavour.

You can make the fabada in a pressure cooker but it is always better to make it over low heat because the flavours bind better and that "chup chup" makes the flavour different, but with the pressure cooker we will save time.
If we decide to cook the fabada in a fast cooker, it should take about 20 minutes (it will also depend on the type of cooker you have, as there are many different types nowadays and some of them cook the dish in 10 minutes).

Fabada is a dish that, according to popular wisdom, "tastes better the day after" it has been cooked. It is usually served in an earthenware dish or casserole with the pieces of cooked compango served separately in a dish. Because of its content, it is a unique dish. There is a dispute among the followers of this dish as to which drink it should be served with: some say cider because of tradition, others say beer, and some say wine. The truth is that there is no drink that is unanimously approved by all. After eating it, it is important to know that faba beans cause gastro-oesophageal reflux, which can be relieved with any suitable medicine to combat stomach heaviness.

Fabada is the most traditional recipe we have in Asturian cuisine. It is undoubtedly one of the best spoon dishes we can find in Spain. Today I present the recipe for easy fabada, with tips and tricks to make it perfect.
First of all, I recommend you buy the best ingredients. Go to a butcher's and they will give you the best meats for this dish, as supermarkets often have it vacuum-packed and it is very industrial. Remember that the better the quality of the ingredients, the better the result.
